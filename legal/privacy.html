1) Data Controller

Your Company (“we”, “us”) is the data controller for personal data processed via this website and our mailing list. If we act as a processor for a client, we will process data only on documented instructions and under a written agreement (GDPR Art. 28 / NDPR Reg. 2.6).

2) Data We Collect

  • Identity/Contact: name, email address (when you subscribe or contact us).
  • Technical: device/browser, IP address, pages visited, timestamps, referral source (used to keep the site secure and improve content).
  • Communications: your messages and preferences (e.g., opt‑in/opt‑out).
  • Cookies: strictly necessary cookies to run the site; optional analytics if you consent.

3) How We Use Data & Legal Bases

We process data only where a lawful basis applies (GDPR Art. 6; NDPR Reg. 2.2):

  • Consent: sending newsletters/updates you requested; placing non‑essential cookies. You can withdraw consent at any time.
  • Legitimate Interests: securing the site, preventing abuse, and understanding aggregate usage (balanced against your rights).
  • Contract: where we provide features or services you signed up for.
  • Legal Obligation: complying with laws and lawful requests.

We do not engage in automated decision‑making that produces legal or similarly significant effects (GDPR Art. 22). If this changes, we will inform you with meaningful information about the logic and effects.

4) Sharing

We share data with vendors who help us operate the Service (e.g., email delivery, analytics). All vendors are bound by agreements requiring confidentiality, security, and compliance with GDPR/NDPR. We do not sell personal data.

5) International Transfers

Where data is transferred outside your country (e.g., EU→non‑EU, Nigeria→other), we use appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), adequacy decisions, or other lawful transfer mechanisms. We assess transfer risks and implement supplementary measures where necessary.

6) Retention

We keep personal data only as long as needed for the purposes above, then delete or irreversibly anonymize it. Typical retention for mailing list data is until you unsubscribe or after 24 months of inactivity, whichever comes first, unless a longer period is required by law or to resolve disputes.

7) Security

We use administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards (access controls, encryption in transit, least‑privilege, logging). No system is perfectly secure; if a breach occurs that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the relevant authority and affected users in line with applicable law.

8) Your Rights (GDPR & NDPR)

  • Access your data and obtain a copy.
  • Rectify inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erase data (“right to be forgotten”) where applicable.
  • Restrict or object to processing, including direct marketing.
  • Port data to another provider (where technically feasible).
  • Withdraw consent at any time (does not affect prior processing).
  • Complain to a data protection authority.

To exercise your rights, email privacy@yourdomain.tld. We will verify your identity and respond within statutory timelines (usually 30 days under NDPR / one month under GDPR).

9) Cookies

We use essential cookies to operate the site. With your consent, we may use analytics cookies to understand aggregate engagement. You can change preferences in our cookie banner or your browser settings.

10) Children

Our Service is not directed to children under 13 (or higher age where local law requires). We do not knowingly collect data from children. If you believe a child provided data, contact us to delete it.

11) Changes

We may update this policy. If changes are material, we will take reasonable steps to notify you. The “Last updated” date shows the latest version.

12) Contact & Complaints

Email: privacy@yourdomain.tld

EU/EEA users may lodge complaints with their local supervisory authority. Nigeria users may contact the Nigerian Data Protection Commission (NDPC). We encourage contacting us first so we can resolve your concern.
